Payment Methods

Stripe should be the primary checkout flow. PayPal can remain as a secondary option. Wallet and card availability depend on device, browser, region, and provider support.

Primary Gateway

Use Stripe Checkout as the default path for one-time payments and memberships. This gives you a hosted secure payment page and a cleaner conversion path.

Payment and billing notice

Payments may be processed through trusted third-party providers. Availability of Stripe, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and card payments depends on provider support, device, browser, location, and technical readiness.

Billing and Renewal Information

Subscription and billing terms should always be shown clearly before checkout.

Billing Disclosure

Plans should be labeled clearly as one-time or recurring. Renewal timing, cancellation process, and billing interval should be visible before purchase.

Permission and Consent

Customers should confirm purchase authorization and, where applicable, consent to automatic renewal before subscribing.

Failed Payment Support

If a recurring payment fails, the account may pause and the customer should receive a billing notice with an update-payment option.

Hosted payment direction

Best practice: do not put raw payment secrets in the frontend. Create Stripe Checkout Sessions on the server and redirect customers to a hosted secure payment page. Use a thank-you page on success and return customers to pricing if they cancel.
