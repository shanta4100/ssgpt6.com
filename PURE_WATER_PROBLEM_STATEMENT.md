# Pure Water Problem Statement
## Defining Global Water Scarcity and Contamination

## The Challenge
The global community faces a dual crisis of water "bankruptcy"—the depletion of accessible fresh water—and systemic contamination. Current infrastructure and agricultural practices have led to a breakdown in natural hydrological cycles, leaving millions without safe access to the most fundamental human requirement.

---

## Core Issue Areas

### 1. Hydrological Disconnection
The separation of surface water management from groundwater recharge has created an imbalance. Rapid runoff and urban expansion prevent the natural infiltration of water, leading to lower water tables and increased drought vulnerability.

### 2. Chemical Saturation
Industrial and agricultural runoff introduces non-biodegradable chemicals into the primary water supply. Existing filtration methods are often reactive rather than preventative, failing to address the source of the toxicity.

### 3. Resource Inequity
Water scarcity is often exacerbated by inefficient distribution and lack of real-time resource visibility. Centralized systems frequently fail to adapt to local ecological shifts or sudden environmental changes.

---

## The Necessity of Intervention
Without a transition to regenerative, chemical-free practices and a technologically enabled management framework, the degradation of global watersheds will become irreversible. This statement serves as the evidentiary basis for the Root Ocean initiative's immediate implementation.

---

## Conclusion
The problem is not merely a lack of water, but a lack of systemic harmony. Restoring this balance requires a shift from extraction to restoration.
