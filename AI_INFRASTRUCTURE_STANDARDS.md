# AI Infrastructure Standards
## Trustless Governance and Autonomous Resource Management

## Overview
This document outlines the technical requirements for the AI layer of the Root Ocean initiative. To ensure individual sovereignty and global reliability, the project utilizes a "Zero-Server" architecture and "Sleeping Agent" logic.

---

## Technical Core Principles

### 1. Tiny AI & Edge Deployment
Infrastructure management is handled by low-power, high-efficiency "Tiny AI" models. These agents reside locally at the point of action (e.g., watershed sensors, irrigation controls) to ensure operation even during network isolation.

### 2. Zero-Server Privacy
No centralized server retains user or regional metadata. All logic is processed locally, ensuring that resource data remains under the jurisdiction of the local watershed management without external exposure.

### 3. Sleeping Agent Protocols
To minimize energy consumption and maximize security, AI agents operate in a "dormant" state, activating only when specific environmental thresholds are met or when a cryptographic handshake is initiated for maintenance.

---

## Data Integrity (Trustless Standards)
* **Immutable Logs:** All resource allocation decisions are recorded on a trustless, decentralized ledger.
* **Cryptographic Verification:** Every sensor node must verify its identity using hardware-level security modules (HSM) before broadcasting data.
* **Non-Adversarial Logic:** The AI objective function is strictly bounded to hydrological restoration and chemical-free agricultural support.

---

## Conclusion
By shifting from centralized "Cloud AI" to decentralized "Tiny AI," the Root Ocean project creates a resilient, unhackable, and sovereign infrastructure for global water management.
