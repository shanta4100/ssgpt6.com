# Hydrological Restoration Protocol
## Systemic Framework for Watershed Recovery

## Overview
This protocol defines the physical interventions required to restore natural water cycles. It focuses on the transition from "drainage-based" infrastructure to "retention-based" hydrology, ensuring groundwater recharge and soil health.

---

## Key Restoration Pillars

### 1. Infiltration-First Management
Current urban and agricultural designs prioritize moving water away as quickly as possible. This protocol mandates the use of permeable surfaces, bioswales, and check dams to slow water down and force it into the subsurface aquifer.

### 2. Watershed Connectivity
Restoration efforts must be viewed as a single, continuous system from the mountain headwaters to the coastal estuaries. We prioritize the removal of ecological blockages to allow for natural sediment transport and biological migration.

### 3. Riparian Buffer Re-establishment
The protection of riverbanks through native vegetation is mandatory. These buffers act as natural filters for nitrates and phosphates, while stabilizing banks against erosion and flooding.

---

## Operational Integration
* **Sensor Calibration:** AI agents (defined in the Infrastructure Standards) monitor soil moisture and flow rates to determine the effectiveness of local recharge zones.
* **Non-Invasive Monitoring:** All restoration progress is tracked via satellite and acoustic sensors to minimize human footprint in sensitive ecological zones.

---

## Conclusion
The goal is the "Root Ocean"—the vast, unseen reservoir of groundwater that sustains life during periods of surface-level drought. By restoring the soil’s ability to hold water, we secure the future of the planet.
